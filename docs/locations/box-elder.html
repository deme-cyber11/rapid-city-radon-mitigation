Protecting Box Elder Families from Elevated Radon Levels

Box Elder is one of the fastest-growing communities in Pennington County, with a population of approximately 10,000 residents. Located just east of Rapid City along Interstate 90, Box Elder is home to Ellsworth Air Force Base — one of the region's largest employers and the home of the B-1B Lancer bomber fleet. The steady influx of military families and civilian workers has driven significant residential construction in the area, but many of these newer homes still face a serious hidden threat: elevated indoor radon levels.

Pennington County is classified by the EPA as Zone 1 for radon risk, meaning homes in Box Elder have a predicted average screening level above 4 pCi/L. The geological composition of the prairie east of the Black Hills includes uranium-bearing shale and clay deposits that produce substantial quantities of radon gas. As this gas migrates upward through the soil, it enters homes through foundation cracks, construction joints, and utility penetrations — regardless of whether the home is brand new or decades old.

Radon Concerns for Military Families at Ellsworth AFB

Military families stationed at Ellsworth Air Force Base often rent or purchase homes in Box Elder's surrounding neighborhoods, including the developments along Radar Hill Road, Liberty Boulevard, and the newer subdivisions near Douglas High School. Because service members typically relocate every few years, many assume they won't be in a home long enough for radon exposure to matter. However, the EPA emphasizes that any level of radon exposure carries risk, and children are especially vulnerable due to their higher breathing rates and developing lungs.

If you're renting in Box Elder, landlords in South Dakota are not required to test for radon, making it your responsibility to ensure your family's safety. Our short-term radon testing can provide results within 48 hours, giving you peace of mind quickly. For homeowners preparing to sell, radon testing and mitigation before listing can prevent delays during the inspection process and protect the sale price.
