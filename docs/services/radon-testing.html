Professional Radon Testing for Rapid City Homes

Radon testing is the only reliable method for determining whether your home has dangerous levels of radon gas. Radon is a naturally occurring, radioactive gas that is produced by the decay of uranium in soil and rock beneath your foundation. It is completely invisible, odorless, and tasteless, which means you cannot detect it without specialized equipment. In Rapid City and across Pennington County, radon is a serious health concern because the Black Hills region sits on uranium-rich granite and Pierre Shale formations that produce radon at rates far above the national average.

The Environmental Protection Agency has established 4 pCi/L (picocuries per liter) as the action level for indoor radon. Any home that tests at or above this threshold should have a mitigation system installed. South Dakota's statewide average indoor radon concentration exceeds 9 pCi/L, more than double the EPA action level, and many Rapid City homes test well above 15 pCi/L. The South Dakota Department of Health recommends that every home in the state be tested regardless of age, foundation type, or neighborhood.

We offer both short-term and long-term radon testing to give you the clearest possible picture of your home's radon exposure. Our certified technicians use continuous radon monitors (CRMs) that record hourly readings over a minimum 48-hour testing window, providing detailed data rather than a simple average. For homeowners who want year-round insight, we also offer long-term alpha track testing over 90 or more days to capture seasonal fluctuations caused by South Dakota's harsh winters and the stack effect.

Short-Term vs. Long-Term Testing

Short-term radon tests are the standard screening method and are the protocol used in virtually all real estate transactions across South Dakota. These tests run for 48 to 96 hours under closed-house conditions and deliver fast, actionable results. They are ideal for homebuyers who need answers before closing, homeowners screening for the first time, and anyone who wants quick confirmation of their home's radon status.

Long-term tests run for 90 days or more and use alpha track detectors to measure your home's average radon concentration across seasons. Because radon levels in the Black Hills can spike during winter months when homes are sealed tight and frozen ground traps gas beneath the foundation, long-term testing is especially valuable for homes that test near the 4 pCi/L action level on a short-term screening.
