Proven Radon Mitigation for Rapid City Homeowners

Radon mitigation is the process of reducing elevated radon gas concentrations inside your home to safe levels. When a radon test reveals levels at or above the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L, a professionally designed and installed mitigation system is the only reliable solution. In Rapid City and across Pennington County, where the average home tests well above that threshold, mitigation is not just recommended — it is essential for protecting your family from long-term radon exposure and the serious health risks associated with it, including lung cancer.

Our team specializes in designing custom radon mitigation systems tailored to your home's specific foundation type, soil conditions, and radon concentration levels. The most common and effective method we use is active sub-slab depressurization (ASD), which involves creating a suction point beneath your concrete slab or basement floor and drawing radon-laden air through sealed PVC piping to a vent above your roofline. This approach prevents radon from ever entering your living space and is the gold-standard method endorsed by the EPA, the American Lung Association, and the South Dakota Department of Health.

We also have experience mitigating homes with crawlspace foundations, slab-on-grade construction, block wall foundations, and combination foundation types. Every home presents unique challenges, and we assess each property individually to determine the most effective system design. Our goal is always the same: bring your radon levels well below 4 pCi/L and verify the results with post-installation testing.

Mitigation Methods We Use

While sub-slab depressurization is the most widely used approach, we employ multiple methods depending on your home's construction:

  • Active Sub-Slab Depressurization (ASD) — The primary method for homes with basements or slab foundations. A radon fan creates negative pressure beneath the slab to intercept gas before it enters.
  • Sub-Membrane Depressurization — Used for crawlspace homes. A heavy-duty vapor barrier is sealed over exposed soil, and suction is applied beneath the membrane.
  • Block Wall Suction — Applied to hollow block foundations where radon can travel through the block cavities. Suction is applied directly to the wall to capture gas inside the blocks.
  • Sealing and Caulking — Supplemental method used alongside active systems. We seal cracks, gaps around pipes, sump lids, and other foundation penetration points to maximize system efficiency.
