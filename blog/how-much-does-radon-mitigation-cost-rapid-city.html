How Much Does Radon Mitigation Cost in Rapid City? (2026 Guide)

Home › Blog › How Much Does Radon Mitigation Cost in Rapid City?

Updated March 2026 · 6 min read

If you live in Rapid City or anywhere in the Black Hills region, radon is not a hypothetical concern — it is a near certainty. South Dakota carries the highest average indoor radon levels in the United States, with a statewide average exceeding 9 pCi/L. That is more than double the EPA action level of 4.0 pCi/L. The granite bedrock beneath the Black Hills naturally contains elevated uranium, and as that uranium decays, radon gas seeps upward through soil and into the foundations of homes across Pennington County.

So when your home tests high, the first question is usually: how much will it cost to fix? Here is what Rapid City homeowners can expect to pay for radon mitigation in 2026, broken down by system type, the factors that influence price, and what a professional installation actually includes.

Average Radon Mitigation Cost in Rapid City (2026)

For most homes in Rapid City, radon mitigation costs between $800 and $1,800. The exact price depends on your foundation type, the size and layout of your home, and how the system needs to be routed. Below is a breakdown by system type:

System Type Typical Cost Notes
Standard sub-slab depressurization $800 – $1,400 Most common for basements & slab-on-grade
Crawl space system $1,200 – $1,800 Requires vapor barrier + fan setup
Multiple suction point system $1,500 – $2,500 Large homes or complex floor plans
Radon testing only $150 – $250 Professional short-term or continuous monitor
Post-mitigation testing Included or $150 – $300 Confirms levels dropped below 4.0 pCi/L

The standard sub-slab depressurization (SSD) system is the gold standard recommended by the EPA and is what the majority of Rapid City homes receive. A pipe is routed from beneath the foundation slab through the home and out the roof, and a continuously running inline fan draws radon gas from beneath the slab and vents it safely above the roofline.

What Affects the Cost of Radon Mitigation?

Several factors shift the price in either direction:

  • Foundation type. Homes with a single basement slab are simplest. Crawl spaces require a sealed vapor barrier before suction works. Homes with both a basement and crawl space may need multiple suction points.
  • Home size and layout. A 1,200-square-foot ranch typically needs one suction point. A 3,000-square-foot split-level with multiple foundation sections may need two or three points for adequate depressurization.
  • Radon concentration. Homes at 6–10 pCi/L often respond to a single suction point. Homes at 20+ pCi/L — not uncommon in Robbinsdale or Canyon Lake — may require a higher-capacity fan or additional suction points.
  • Pipe routing. If the pipe can run through a closet or utility chase to the roof, installation is straightforward. Exterior wall routing with paint-matched siding increases labor and materials.
  • Accessibility. Tight crawl spaces, finished basements with no exposed slab access, or complex HVAC ductwork beneath the floor can add labor time.
  • Seasonal demand. Winter is peak season for radon mitigation in Rapid City — cold, dry conditions mean homes are sealed tight, concentrating radon indoors. Scheduling in spring or early fall may offer shorter wait times.

What Is Included in a Professional Radon Mitigation Installation?

When you hire a licensed radon mitigation company in Rapid City, a standard installation typically includes:

  • Pre-installation assessment — foundation inspection and sub-slab communication testing to determine the best suction point location
  • Core drilling — a 4–5 inch hole through the basement slab for the suction point
  • PVC piping — Schedule 40 PVC routed from the suction point through the home and out through the roof
  • Inline radon fan — a continuously running fan rated for residential radon mitigation
  • Sealing — cracks, gaps, and sump pit covers sealed to prevent radon bypass
  • U-tube manometer — a visual indicator confirming the fan is creating suction
  • Post-mitigation testing — conducted 24–48 hours after installation to confirm levels dropped below 4.0 pCi/L (often included at no extra charge)

Ongoing Costs After Installation

Radon mitigation is not a one-time fix and forget. The fan runs 24/7, and there are modest ongoing costs:

  • Electricity: $5–$15 per month ($150–$300 per year), depending on fan wattage
  • Re-testing: the EPA recommends testing every 2 years to confirm the system remains effective ($150–$250)
  • Fan replacement: quality radon fans last 10–20 years; replacement runs $300–$600 including installation

For most Rapid City homeowners, total annual operating cost is under $200 — a modest price for continuous protection against the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States.

How to Choose a Radon Mitigation Company in Rapid City

Not all radon contractors are equal. When evaluating companies in the Rapid City area, look for these indicators:

  • State certification. South Dakota does not license radon professionals at the state level, so look for NRPP (National Radon Proficiency Program) or NRSB (National Radon Safety Board) certification.
  • Insurance. General liability and workers’ compensation insurance protect you if something goes wrong during installation.
  • Post-mitigation testing. A reputable company includes or offers follow-up testing to prove their system works.
  • Written warranty. Look for a guarantee that the system will reduce your radon level below 4.0 pCi/L, with a commitment to adjust the system if it does not.
  • Local experience. The Black Hills has unique geology. A contractor familiar with Rapid City foundations, soil conditions, and the granite bedrock will design a more effective system than a national franchise working from a template.

Why Rapid City Homeowners Should Not Wait

Radon is an invisible, odorless gas whose health effects accumulate over years. The EPA estimates radon causes approximately 21,000 lung cancer deaths annually in the United States, and South Dakota’s geology puts Black Hills residents at disproportionate risk. At $800–$1,800, mitigation is a one-time investment that protects your household for decades. And if you are buying or selling a home in Rapid City, radon testing is now a standard part of most real estate transactions in Pennington County — a functioning system with documented results removes a common negotiation hurdle.
