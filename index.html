Why Rapid City Homeowners Need Radon Mitigation

Rapid City sits at the edge of the Black Hills, one of the most radon-prone geological regions in North America. The uranium-bearing granite and metamorphic rocks that give the Black Hills their dramatic landscape also produce significant radon gas as uranium decays naturally in the soil and bedrock. That gas migrates upward through the ground, through foundation cracks and gaps, and into the air inside your home — where it can accumulate to dangerous concentrations without any visible sign.

South Dakota's cold winters make the problem worse. When homeowners seal their houses against the weather, radon levels rise because there's less ventilation to dilute the gas. Many Rapid City families spend the most time indoors during the very months when radon concentrations are highest. This combination of high geological radon production and extended winter home sealing is why South Dakota leads the nation in average indoor radon levels by a significant margin.

Badlands Radon installs sub-slab depressurization systems that address the problem at its source — creating negative pressure beneath your foundation so radon gas is routed outside before it enters your living space. Our systems are sized and designed for the specific conditions in your home, and we test after installation to confirm the results meet EPA safety guidelines. Don't assume your home is fine — test it, and if levels are elevated, let us fix it. Call (605) 640-5642 for a free estimate.
